# Tomato Mosaic Virus

## Definition
**Tomato Mosaic Virus** (ToMV) is a viral disease that affects tomato plants, caused by the *Tobamovirus*. This virus can lead to significant yield loss and affect the quality of the fruit.

## Symptoms
1. **Leaf Symptoms**:
   - Mottled light and dark green (mosaic) pattern on leaves.
   - Leaves may be small, curled, and distorted.
   - Stunted growth and reduced vigor.

2. **Fruit Symptoms**:
   - Uneven ripening and internal browning.
   - Reduced fruit size and quality.
   - Occasionally, fruits may show mottling or streaking.

3. **Plant Symptoms**:
   - Overall stunted growth.
   - Reduced yield due to poor fruit development.

## Treatment
1. **Resistant Varieties**:
   - Planting tomato varieties that are resistant to Tomato Mosaic Virus can help reduce the incidence of the disease.

2. **Seed Treatment**:
   - Use pathogen-free seeds or treat seeds with a bleach solution to reduce the risk of infection.

3. **Sanitation**:
   - Remove and destroy infected plants and debris to reduce sources of viral inoculum.
   - Clean tools and equipment with a disinfectant to prevent the spread of the virus.

4. **Cultural Practices**:
   - Avoid working with wet plants to reduce the risk of spreading the virus.
   - Control weeds that can host the virus.

5. **Crop Rotation**:
   - Rotate crops with non-host plants to reduce viral buildup in the soil.

6. **Monitoring**:
   - Regularly inspect plants for early signs of infection.
   - Remove and destroy infected plants promptly to prevent the spread of the virus.
